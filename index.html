Dot Matrix Printer Fonts

LA100

The DEC LA100 Letterwriter and Letterprinter had the best dot-matrix printer draft quality font ever, a serif-ish font. To my knowledge they were the only impact printer models, ever, to have that style of font. This is my tribute to it.

The LA100 font only covers printable ASCII characters from space (0x20) to tilde (0x7e). My sources were actual printouts from actual LA100 printers from 25 to 30 years ago. Online printout examples are nigh impossible to find, and I was not able to find even complete ASCII coverage.

LA120

The font used on the DECwriter III and DECprinter III (LA120) is a pretty good font if you want the overall style of what most dot matrix printers' draft quality fonts looked like.

The LA100 font covers printable ASCII characters from space (0x20) to tilde (0x7e) as well as a few ISO-8859-1 extensions and whatnots. Officially, only ASCII is supported. My sources are various online printouts. The letters with descenders look kind of goofy because these models had seven pins on their printheads, as opposed to the LA100's nine pins.

About

Fonts are available at the following "sizes":

  • 12 characters per inch, or 96 characters per line in a normal letter-size fanfold configuration.
    (1 character = 0.08333 inch)
  • 10 characters per inch, or 80 characters per line.
    (1 character = 0.1 inch)
  • 16.5 characters per inch, or 132 characters per line.
    (1 character = 0.06060 inch)

For best experience:

  • CSS: specify 24px
  • macOS: specify 24pt
  • Windows: specify 18pt
